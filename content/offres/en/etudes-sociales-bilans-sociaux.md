# Social Studies and Workforce Reports

**Turning your workforce data into a management tool — not just a compliance obligation.**

The workforce report, gender equality index, comparative situation report, OETH disability employment indicators, senior employment reports: the production of social studies has grown both in complexity and in volume. The risk is twofold: producing compliant but unused documents, or producing unusable documents because the underlying data is unreliable.

GBA Connect supports you in the **rigorous production and management-level use** of your social studies.

## Who this offer is for

- You need to produce your **workforce report** or **mandatory returns** and lack the methodology or internal resources to do so
- You want to **strengthen the reliability of social indicators** circulating in the organisation (headcount, payroll cost, absenteeism, turnover, gender balance)
- You are facing a **specific challenge** (low gender equality index, CSR initiative, strategic collective negotiation) that requires in-depth social analysis
- You want to **industrialise the annual production** of your studies through your HRIS

## What we deliver

- **Scope definition**: mandatory indicators, internal management indicators, CSR indicators, sources and methodologies
- **Source data reliability**: quality audit, gap identification, remediation plan
- **Production of the studies**: workforce report, gender equality index, comparative situation report, other mandatory or voluntary reports
- **Analysis and commentary**: interpretation of indicators, historical benchmarking, sector comparisons, alert points
- **Tailored deliverables**: versions for management, for the Works Council (CSE), for external publication
- **Recurring production procedure**: calendar, sources, controls, validation, possible automation
